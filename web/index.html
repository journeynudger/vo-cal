What it does

Three pillars. That's the whole product.

Not a hundred features you'll never open. Vo-Cal does the three things that actually change whether you keep tracking — and deliberately leaves the rest out.

01 — Voice-first logging

Just say it

"A PB&J and a banana." Vo-Cal parses every item, maps it to a real food database, and logs the calories and macros — no searching, no scrolling lists.

02 — Personalized protocol

A plan built around your real life

A deeper intake than any other app — your work, routine, obligations, stress, and meds — produces a starting target that's actually right. You feel seen, not surveyed.

03 — Situational nudging

Nudges that find you mid-week

Not an end-of-week report. A short, human check-in during the week — "rough day? want to repeat Tuesday?" — that keeps a hard stretch from running off the rails.

What it leaves out — on purpose

Tracks five things. Nags about none.

The dashboard shows calories, protein, produce, fiber, and water. That's it — no saturated-fat scolding, no sodium pop-ups, no planning mazes. Watching sugar or sodium? Add it yourself; it's off by default. Vo-Cal is the essential slice of a food tracker, on purpose.

It hears the details

A photo can't tell light mayo from full-fat.

Calories hide inside the things a camera can't see — the fat ratio on the beef, whether the cheddar was reduced-fat, the olive oil in the pan. When one of those actually moves your numbers, Vo-Cal asks. When it doesn't, it stays quiet.

  • One quick tap per ingredient that matters. Whole, reduced-fat, or fat-free? Light or regular mayo? Answer or skip.
  • Honest confidence on every meal. The number you see comes with how sure the app is — no false precision.
  • "Log anyway" is always there. In a hurry? Take the typical values and move on.

Questions

The honest FAQ.

How is this different from MyFitnessPal or Cal AI?
You speak instead of search. MyFitnessPal grew bloated and paywalled the basics; Vo-Cal is the essential slice, by subtraction. Cal AI reads photos — but a camera can't see the fat ratio on the beef or whether the cheddar was reduced-fat. Your voice can say it, so Vo-Cal asks only when a detail actually changes your numbers.
Do I have to use voice, or can I type?
Voice is the star — it's how logging drops to one breath. But text search over the same food library is always there as the fallback for when speaking isn't an option.
What does the dashboard actually show?
Five things: calories left, protein, produce, fiber, and water. Carbs, fats, sugar, and sodium are off by default — no unsolicited nagging. If you genuinely need to watch one (say sugar as a diabetic, or sodium for blood pressure), you add it yourself on an edit screen.
How does the personalized protocol work?
A deeper, more human intake — your occupation, routine, obligations, stress, and any medications that affect hunger — feeds a coach's formulas to produce a real starting target for calories, protein, water, and fiber. It infers your activity from your actual life rather than asking you to rate yourself, and it recalibrates as you change. It is not medical advice.
Are my food log and voice private?
Yes. Everything is scoped to your account, your transcription runs on-device, and your diary and recordings aren't shared. Any internal access during the beta is audit-logged.
When can I use it, and on what?
Vo-Cal is a native iOS app, arriving first as a TestFlight private beta for coaches and their clients. It hasn't opened yet — request access below and we'll reach out as spots open.
